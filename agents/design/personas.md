# Personas

Status: DONE
Owner: UX & Design Agent
Tool mode: repo_only

## Objective

Define the primary buyer profiles so design, content, and flow decisions reflect real renovation concerns rather than generic marketing assumptions.

## Persona 1: Apartment owner in a new build

- Profile:
  - owns or is preparing to receive a new-build apartment
  - needs engineering work, rough works, and finishing coordinated from scratch
- Goals:
  - understand full scope
  - avoid fragmented contractors
  - estimate timing and next steps quickly
- Concerns:
  - hidden engineering mistakes
  - poor sequencing between trades
  - unclear accountability
- What this person needs on the site:
  - a clear process
  - turnkey service explanation
  - direct CTA for project briefing

## Persona 2: House owner planning a phased renovation

- Profile:
  - renovating a private house or cottage
  - may need staged work over a longer timeline
- Goals:
  - understand whether the team can manage complex scope
  - see supervision and procurement support
  - confirm geographic fit and project control
- Concerns:
  - coordination complexity
  - supplier and delivery issues
  - cost of mistakes across multiple systems
- What this person needs on the site:
  - strong service coverage
  - supervision and procurement pages
  - proof of structure and accountability

## Persona 3: Secondary-market apartment buyer

- Profile:
  - purchased an older apartment
  - expects demolition, infrastructure replacement, and finishing
- Goals:
  - understand what needs replacement versus cosmetic updates
  - compare selective stage work versus full renovation
  - get to a concrete conversation quickly
- Concerns:
  - hidden defects
  - outdated electrical and plumbing systems
  - cost overruns and scope creep
- What this person needs on the site:
  - rough works and engineering detail
  - FAQ on renovation stages
  - brief-driven contact flow

## Shared user motivations

- reduce risk
- reduce coordination burden
- understand sequence and scope
- trust that one team can handle the full path
- move from browsing to a concrete project discussion

## Shared design implications

- the homepage must state full-scope renovation capability immediately
- service breadth should be visible without overwhelming the screen
- process and trust signals must appear before deep scrolling fatigue
- CTAs should ask for project details, not generic callbacks
- mobile scanning must be fast and calm, not text-heavy
